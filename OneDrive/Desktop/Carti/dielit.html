Background and Release

Die Lit is the debut studio album by American rapper Playboi Carti, released on May 11, 2018 via AWGE and Interscope Records. The album consists of 19 tracks and features guest appearances from Skepta, Travis Scott, Lil Uzi Vert, Pi'erre Bourne, Nicki Minaj, Bryson Tiller, Chief Keef, Gunna, Red Coldhearted, Young Thug, and Young Nudy. Production was executively handled by Pi'erre Bourne, alongside other record producers such as Don Cannon, IndigoChildRick, Maaly Raw, Art Dealer, and Playboi Carti himself. Die Lit serves as the follow-up to Carti's self‑titled 2017 mixtape.

In December 2017, a video showed Carti behind a mixing board with the caption "Album Mode." In March 2018, Pi'erre Bourne teased via Twitter that the project had been finalized. On May 2, 2018, Carti released "Love Hurts" (feat. Travis Scott) as a promotional single exclusively on SoundCloud, and on May 10, 2018, he announced the album's release date via Twitter. ASAP Rocky also revealed the album’s artwork on the same day. Initially released exclusively on Tidal, Die Lit later appeared on other streaming services.

Critical Reception

Die Lit received generally positive reviews from critics. It holds a Metacritic score of 71/100 based on seven reviews. Critics praised the album's infectious production and Carti’s unique style. For example, Pitchfork described it as “an album that works almost completely from its own lunatic script,” while Tiny Mix Tapes noted its potential as a collection of party records—though some reviewers mentioned that its minimalism could limit its longevity.

Commercial Performance

Die Lit debuted at number three on the US Billboard 200, earning 61,000 album‑equivalent units in its first week (including 5,000 pure album sales). On July 31, 2019, the album was certified gold by the RIAA for combined sales and streams exceeding 500,000 units. As of January 2021, it has earned approximately 1.1 million album‑equivalent units and accumulated 1.67 billion on‑demand streams.
